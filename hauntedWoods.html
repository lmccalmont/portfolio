Haunted Woods

The assignment

After spending about half the semester going through Unity tutorials, we were given a broad assignment: create any VR experience that we wish. Being a fan of horror, I pretty quickly thought of making a horror game.

Game Concept

Player is camping in the woods when they hear a noise. They decide it's best to get out of the woods, but they cannot find their car keys. While being chased by a ghost spider that only lurks in the dark, the player must find their keys and their car to escape. In other words, light sources are safe areas.

Beginning Work

Once my project partner and I were happy with the game concept, we knew that with the set deadline we had to approach this with a plan. We made a list of what was necessary for the game function and what would be “nice to have”. A rough timeline of how the game was developed is:

  • Continuous movement
  • Importing assets we thought would be useful
  • Monster (spider) that followed player
  • Spider that would de-spawn near light source and "kill" player if collided with
  • Respawning spider
  • Random spawning truck and key
  • Game over logic (both win and lose)
  • Audio
  • UI & finishing touches

Final Result

Both my project partner and I are proud of the result. This was both of our first time creating any type of game or working with VR. We met all of our “must-have” objectives and even some extra. The player is chased through the woods by a ghost spider. The player can explore and find a car key and then get to the pickup truck (which both spawn is a few random locations each game) to escape. Light sources

I learned a ton about Unity, C#, and Virtual Reality and really enjoyed the creative process.
